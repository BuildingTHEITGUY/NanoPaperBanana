Abstract

Despite rapid advances in research methodologies at the University of Dubai, generating publication-ready illustrations remains a labor-intensive bottleneck in our research workflow. To address this challenge, we are implementing PaperBanana, an agentic framework for automated generation of publication-ready academic illustrations.

Powered by state-of-the-art Vision-Language Models and image generation technologies, PaperBanana will provide University of Dubai researchers with specialized agents to retrieve references, plan content and style, render images, and iteratively refine illustrations via self-critique.

This implementation directly supports our university's research excellence initiatives by significantly reducing the time researchers spend creating visualizations while enhancing the visual quality and consistency of our academic publications across all faculties.

Method Overview

We propose PaperBanana, a reference-driven agentic framework. As illustrated below, PaperBanana orchestrates a collaborative team of five specialized agents—Retriever, Planner, Stylist, Visualizer, and Critic.



  • Retriever Agent: Identifies relevant reference examples.
  • Planner Agent: Acts as the cognitive core, translating context into detailed descriptions.
  • Stylist Agent: Ensures adherence to academic aesthetic standards (UD Branding compatible).
  • Visualizer Agent: Transforms descriptions into visual output or executable code.
  • Critic Agent: Inspects generated images/plots for feedback and refinement.

Benchmark Construction

We use PaperBananaBench, a dedicated benchmark curated from NeurIPS methodology diagrams, to ensure our output meets the highest international standards.

Experimental Results

Our method consistently outperforms leading baselines in faithfulness, conciseness, readability, and aesthetics.
